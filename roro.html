We explore nice places on SNS, ask about them, save them on the map app, and visit. However, this flow has cut while you move around various services. Also, the place we are looking for is not just a point. We consume a wide area, such as a restaurant and shopping place nearby cafe, the atmosphere of an alley, or special experience that fits my taste. RORO is a new space where you can fully enjoy the alleyway culture of GEN-Z.

BACKGROUND

Alley culture has been representative context showing the generations so far. GEN-Z finds a nice alley in SNS and visits by map services. However, even if they go to the same alley, they consume it according to their taste. For GEN-Z, alleys have become more subdivided and personal spaces.

A Case Study on the Local Platform from the Public Design Perspective for the Revitalization of the Alley Commercial District, Choi Yoo-sik (2021)

Place attachment psychology is hidden in how the GEN-Z consumes alleys.

PERSONA JOURNEY MAP

Based on the characteristics of these GEN-Z, we made persona and journey maps. Persona was able to divide into primary persona DIGGER, which actively searches for places, and secondary persona FOLLOWER, which sees and follows DIGGER's content. As a result of comparing based on the Journey Map, the experiences of the two users were not completely separated, and everyone could become a DIGGER or a FOLLOEWER in the alley visit process.

SERVICE CONCEPT

GEN-Z's alley experiences are cyclical; exploring, visiting, and sharing. However, the services they use do not fully reflect this flow of experience. We suggest the BRAND NEW WAY FOR GEN-Z’S ALLEYWAY EXPERIENCE

An integral circulation structure in which everyone becomes a DIGGER or a FOLLOWER during the alley visit process.
